Lion's Club came to Friendly, WV in 1959, due to generous sponsorship by the Sistersville Lions Club. The First Charter President was James McIntyre, Sr and held that position for two years before becoming a long time Club Secretary until his passing in the late 1990's.

Adhering to our motto of "We Serve", we provide service to those who cannot serve themselves. Our primary focus is eyesight preservation. We provide monies for people to get eye exams and possible glasses on a case by case basis.

Our two main charities, other than our local charities, are the West Virginia Lions Sight Conservation Foundation and Lions Clubs International Foundation. The Sight Foundation provides monies for people to get further vision and hearing assistance though the WVU Eye Institute that are way more than a local Club an afford. Lions Clubs International Foundation provides monies to natural and manmade disasters all over the world. Since 1979, LCIF has provided nearly $2 million to assistance to West Virginia.

For each member in a Club, they provide assistance to 50 people locally and world wide. Since July, 2017, Lions Clubs International has provided service in some way or form to 200 million people all over the world. We are the largest service organization in the world with 1.4 million members in 200 countries and territories all over the world.

We were founded by an insurance salesman in Chicago by the name of Melvin Jones in 1917. He and a group of his business cohorts formed an organization that serves people.
